Assessment Insight: Worth Index

These twenty-six items are designed to ascertain your perceptions about human worth. There are no right or wrong answers, since people’s perceptions differ.

Instructions

Use the 7-point scale to indicate the intensity of your perception about each statement. Mark number 1 if you very strongly disagree with the statement and a 7 if you very strongly agree with the statement. Use numbers 2 through 6 to indicate varying degrees of your disagreement or agreement.

1=very strongly disagree 2=strongly disagree 3=mildly disagree 4=neutral 5=mildly agree 6=strongly agree 7=very strongly agree

PLEASE give your honest, immediate response.

Scoring the Worth Index

Each respondent receives two scores, one for the conditional perception and one for the unconditional items. The numbers of the items corresponding to each perception and within each subscale are given in table 2.1. A high score of 84 is possible for each perception. The lowest score possible is 12. The person with the highest unconditional perception would score 84/12. The person with the highest conditional perception would score 12/84.

The descriptors given in table 2.2, high, high moderate, moderate, low moderate, and low and their accompanying scores indicate the strength of the respondent’s perception. If a respondent’s perception is clear, the score should be high on one scale and low on the other, toward the 84/12 or 12/84. If the scores on both of the scales are similar, e.g., 42/42 or 15/18, there is no clear distinction whether the perception of worth is unconditional or conditional. A more complete understanding of a respondent’s perception is attained by analyzing the four subscale scores within each of the two perspectives, also given in table 2.2.
